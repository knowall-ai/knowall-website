Stage 1: Proto-Sprint

Purpose

Designed to test ideas quickly and reduce the risk of a larger project. It produces a working, non-production prototype and gives all stakeholders a clear understanding of the next stages — aligning expectations, exposing risks early and establishing a shared vision. An ideal precursor to a project.

Delivery

An intensive 4-day journey from idea to prototype using the Microsoft Power Platform and related technologies (Copilot / AI). Applying Agile and DevOps principles, we move rapidly through Prep > Design > Engineer > Test, with real-time in-person collaboration and feedback loops.

  • Detailed visual mock-ups of key functions and UI design
  • A prototype demonstrating core functionality as proof of concept
  • A clear roadmap for the next development phases
  • A refined product backlog and supporting process documentation

The T-Minus-15 Lifecycle

♾️ A continuous Dev & Ops loop, from prep to production

Prep→ Design→ Engineer→ Test→ Operate

1 · Prep

Deep understanding of the business, and a backlog of user requirements.

2 · Design

Solution design including technical architecture, data models and UX/UI designs.

3 · Engineer

Rapid delivery according to the Prep and Design.

4 · Test

Quality review to ensure the solution meets the original requirements.

5 · Operate

Continual integration and continuous deployments to operations to ensure delivery of value.

Built on T-Minus-15

T-Minus-15 is our open-source methodology to deliver projects, seamlessly blending the best principles of Agile and DevOps. At its heart we prioritise collaboration and transparency — daily stand-ups welcome our clients, ensuring swift issue resolution and alignment with project objectives.

A structured approach following Prep > Design > Engineer > Test > Operate keeps delivery efficient and effective, with a laser-sharp focus on high-quality solutions that meet your exact needs. We take pride in delivering to Production at the earliest opportunity, gathering valuable feedback from stakeholders and end-users — an iterative approach that consistently meets and exceeds expectations.
